Understanding Fuel Cost Calculations

Calculating fuel costs for your trip involves key factors: distance, vehicle fuel efficiency (mileage), and current fuel price. Our calculator uses the formula: Fuel Required = Distance ÷ Mileage, and Total Cost = Fuel Required × Fuel Price.

For example, a 300 km trip in a car with 15 km/l efficiency and petrol at ₹100/liter needs 20 liters costing ₹2,000 total, with ₹6.67 per kilometer cost.

Petrol vehicles offer good performance but higher costs. Diesel vehicles provide better mileage for long trips. CNG vehicles are most economical with lower per-kilometer costs.

Tips to Reduce Fuel Costs

  • Maintain proper tire pressure to improve fuel efficiency by up to 3%
  • Remove excess weight - every 100 kg increases consumption by 6-8%
  • Use AC wisely - open windows at low speeds, AC at highway speeds
  • Plan routes to avoid traffic and reduce idle time
  • Regular maintenance including oil changes and air filter replacement
  • Drive smoothly with gradual acceleration and braking

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How accurate is this fuel cost calculator?
A: The calculator provides estimates based on your input. Actual consumption may vary due to driving conditions, vehicle condition, and driving habits.
Q: What factors affect vehicle mileage?
A: Mileage is affected by engine condition, tire pressure, vehicle weight, driving speed, traffic conditions, and maintenance quality.
Q: Should I use city or highway mileage figures?
A: Use the mileage that best represents your planned driving conditions. For mixed driving, use an average of city and highway mileage.
